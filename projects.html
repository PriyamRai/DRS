Safety Precautions

Flood

What to do before a flood

Be informed by receiving alerts, warnings and public safety information.
Create a communications plan so your family will know how to connect during an emergency.
Assemble an emergency kit.
Be Proactive: Leave before the flooding starts to avoid getting stranded.

What to do during a flood

Stay informed: Tune into your local news for updates on flooding in progress.
Follow evacuation orders and heed warning signs.
Avoid floodwaters: It is NEVER safe to drive or walk throug them.

What to do after a flood

Stay Informed: Tune into your local news for updates on affected areas and the safety of your drinking water.
Avoid floodwaters: Standing water can hide chemicals that can make you sick, power lines that can cause electrocution and sharp debris that can seriously harm you.
Avoid disaster areas: Your presence may hamper emergency operations.
Contact your family and loved ones to let them know you are okay.

Earthquake

What to do before an Earthquake

Place large and heavy objects on lower shelves.
Store breakable items in low, closed cabinnets with latches.
Hang heavy items away form places where people sit/sleep.
Repair defective electrical wiring and leaky gas connections.

What to do during an Earthquake

DROP to the ground, take cover by getting under a sturdy table and HOLD ON until the shaking stops.
Try to stay calm as possible and DO NOT PANIC.

What to do after an Earthquake

Avoid entering damaged buildings or structures.
Do not light matchsticks or gas.
Use stairs instead of elevators.
Call out for medical help in case you see any injured person.

Fire

Act immediately but try to stay calm.
Do not attempt to extinguish a fire unless you are trained to do so.
To waken anyone who may be asleep, shout!
Use the stairs- NEVER use elevators.
Put a wet cloth under doors to help prevent the spread of smoke.
Hold your breath. Move quickly, covering head and hair.
If clothing catches fire, stop where you are. Drop to the ground, and cover your mouth and face with your hands to protect them from the flames.
Never get into a lift when there is a fire. Use the stairs.
If your building is on fire, unless you are trapped, get out, stay out and call the fire service on 101 immediately.

Landslides

Stay calm and alert.
Follow evacuation orders and instructions form local authorities.
Stay from steep slopes, cliffs, or hillsides.
Stay informed through local news updates and emergency broadcasts.
Familiarize yourself with signs of an impending landslide.
Pack some long lasting foods.
Check for injured or trapped indiviuals and provide assistance if possible.
Report any gas leaks, electircal hazards, or damaged utility lines to authorities.

Volcanic Erruption

Know your Risk

Know if you are in an area at risk. There are many potentially active volcanoes in INDIA, including Barren Island, Narcondam Island, Deccan Traps, Baratang, Dhinodhar Hill, Dhosi Hill, Tosham Hills etc.

Plan to Stay Connected and Safe

Listen to local emergency information and follow instruction from local officials.
Create a support team to help you prepare and stay safe.
Learn about your community's plan and then make plans with your household.
The only way to stay safe from a blast, flying debris, hot gases, fast-moving mud, rock, water and lava flow is to evacuate if local officials advise.
Use N-95 masks to help protected against volcanic ash and gases.
Avoid driving and stay in home.
Gather food, water and medicine. Organize supplies into a Go-Kit and a Stay-at-Home kit.
Have at least one gallon of water per peson per day.
Keep personal, financial and medical records safe and easy to access.

Nuclear Explosion

What to do to be prepared

Identify the best shelter location near you. The best locations are underground and in the middle of larger buildings.
Make sure you have an Emergency Supply Kit to stay atleast 24 hours.

What to do: During Survive

If warned of an imminent attack, immediately get inside the nearest building and move away from windows.
If you are outdoors when a detonation occurs take cover from the blast behind anything that might offer protection.
Stay tuned for updated instuctions from emergency response officials.
