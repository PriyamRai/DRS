General Responsibilities of Volunteers

- Volunteers are responsible for reviewing resource requests from users and coordinating the distribution of resources, such as food, water, medical supplies, and shelter materials.
- Volunteers must be prepared to respond rapidly to emergency alerts, engage in search and rescue operations, and provide immediate assistance to those in distress.
- Volunteers are responsible for clear and timely communication with users and other volunteers. This includes responding to chat messages and queries promptly.
- Volunteers engage in community outreach, spreading awareness about the Disaster Relief Platform and its services. They may also educate communities about disaster preparedness and safety measures.
- Some volunteers may receive training to provide crisis counseling and emotional support to individuals affected by disasters. This is critical for the mental and emotional well-being of disaster survivors.
- Given the traumatic experiences that disaster survivors may face, volunteers must approach their work with sensitivity and empathy. Providing emotional support is an important aspect of their role.
- Volunteers often collaborate with other relief organizations, government agencies, and local authorities to create a unified and coordinated response to disasters.
